How to Score Tennis Games

Tennis is scored in three metrics: games, sets, and matches. The game score increments differently from set and match scores; it goes from 0 (spoken as "love"), 15, 30, 40, and game. When a player wins a point, their game score increases to the next increment. If a player wins a point while their score is 40, they win the game. However, if both players have a score of 40, it’s called deuce. To win the game when the score is deuce, a player must win two points in a row. When they win one point at deuce, their score changes to AD. If the other player wins the point while a player’s score is AD, the score resets to deuce. This repeats until a player wins two points in a row.

How to Score Tennis Sets and Matches

Once a player wins a game, their set score increases by one. To win a set, a player must win six games with a difference of two games between the two players’ set scores. After winning a set, the player's match score increases by one. To win the match, a player must win two sets to have a match score of two.

How to Use the ScoreSense Microphone

At the bottom of the Score Page below the game score buttons, there are two buttons that operate the ScoreSense microphone. The left button begins the recording and recognizes everything the user says. After pressing the left button, it is wise to wait about five seconds before speaking as it takes the function time to prepare the recording procedure. After specifying which player won the point to the microphone, either “home” or “visitor”, click the right button to stop recording. A point will then be added to the specified player’s game score. To see what the microphone registered when speaking, refer to the microphone feedback text below the two microphone buttons.
